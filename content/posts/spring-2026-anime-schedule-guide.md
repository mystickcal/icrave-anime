## Early Confirmed Spring 2026 Anime

Here are the shows officially confirmed for Spring 2026 so far:

**Ascendance of a Bookworm Season 4** is finally happening. After the cliffhanger ending of Season 3, we're getting more of Myne's journey through the book-obsessed world. If you've been following the light novels, you know exactly why this season is going to hit different. For newcomers, [check out what made Winter 2026's sequels so good](/posts/winter-2026-anime-sequels-guide/) to see why continuation seasons are crushing it right now.

**Himekishi wa Barbaroi no Yome** (The Princess Knight Becomes the Barbarian's Bride) is bringing historical romance with a twist. A princess knight forced into a political marriage with a barbarian king sounds like standard fare until you realize both characters are actually competent adults. Refreshing, right?

**Reincarnation no Kaben** (Reincarnation no Kaben) adds another isekai to the mix, but this one's focused on music and reincarnation across different eras. The manga's art style is gorgeous, so I'm curious how the animation studio handles it.

**Kami no Niwatsuki Kusunoki-tei** brings slice-of-life supernatural elements together. Think traditional Japanese inn meets spirit world visitors. Perfect comfort watch material.

**Koko wa Ore ni Makasete Saki ni Ike to Itte kara 10-nen ga Tattara Densetsu ni Natteita** (I Said Leave This to Me and Go, and 10 Years Later I Became a Legend) gives us that classic "overpowered protagonist returns" setup. A hero who stayed behind to hold off enemies discovers a decade later that he's become a myth. 

**Kanan-sama wa Akumade Choroi** rounds out the confirmed list with a rom-com about a supposedly cunning student council president who's actually easy to read. School setting, misunderstandings, and comedy hijinks guaranteed.

Several other titles are floating around in "likely Spring 2026" territory, but I'm waiting for official studio confirmations before adding them to my calendar.
